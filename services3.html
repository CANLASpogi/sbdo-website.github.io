OUR SERVICES

PROPHYLAXIS AND PERIODONTICS

  • Scaling

    • Scaling is a dental cleaning procedure that specifically targets the removal of dental plaque and tartar (calculus) from the surfaces of teeth. Dental plaque is a soft, sticky film that forms on teeth and contains bacteria. If plaque is not removed through regular oral hygiene practices such as brushing and flossing, it can mineralize and harden into tartar.

    If dental plaque is not regularly removed through effective oral hygiene practices, it can lead to various oral health issues and contribute to the development of dental problems.

    This cleaning procedure is typically performed using specialized instruments, such as ultrasonic scalers or hand scalers. It involves carefully scraping away the plaque and tartar from the tooth surfaces, including both above and below the gumline. This helps prevent and treat gum diseases like gingivitis and periodontitis.

  • Deep Scaling/Periodontal Treatment

    • Deep scaling which is also known as deep cleaning or root planing, is a dental procedure that goes beyond the standard dental cleaning to treat more advanced stages of gum disease, such as periodontitis. Periodontitis is characterized by inflammation and infection of the gums, which can lead to the loss of supporting bone around the teeth if left untreated.

    Deep scaling typically involves two main components in its' procedure.

    1. Scaling

    Similar to the standard scaling procedure, the dental professional removes plaque and tartar from the tooth surfaces, both above and below the gumline. This is performed using specialized instruments and tools like ultrasonic scalers and hand scalers.

    2. Root Planing

    This step involves smoothing the surfaces of the tooth roots to remove bacterial toxins and promote the reattachment of the gums to the teeth. This helps eliminate rough areas on the roots where bacteria can collect.

    The goal of deep scaling is to reduce inflammation, control infection, and prevent further progression of gum disease. Deep scaling is an essential part of periodontal treatment and is usually followed by more frequent maintenance cleanings to manage and maintain the condition of the patient's dental and oral health.
